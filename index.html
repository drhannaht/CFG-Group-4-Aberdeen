Book of the week

Normal People

By Sally Rooney

❤️❤️❤️❤️❤️

On the surface, Sally Rooney’s ‘Normal People’ is a classic will-they-won’t-they love story that follows Irish teenagers Marianne and Connell as they navigate identity, relationships, and growing up. However the novel also has a darker underbelly; exploring themes of domestic and physical abuse, depression, and co-dependency. The fluid story-telling feels like a friend updating you on their love life, each chapter a new instalment that starts a few months on from the previous one and fills in the gap in a non-linear fashion. Rooney’s secret is her ability to have the reader rooting for Connell and Marianne’s relationship, not because of the near-misses and inability to communicate their feelings (of which these moments were plentiful), but rather by building a relationship which is strong at the very foundation. Whether being on the peripherals of each other's lives, isolated by betrayal or toxic partners, or being by each other's sides, madly in love, the couple’s ability to understand one another is what makes this love story worth reading.
